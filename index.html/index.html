Sisters are Doing It For Themselves
Project Title
Lefty Lucy Mobile App
Executive Summary
Our conceptual app is designed to empower women and marginalized groups to plan and complete home repair projects themselves by providing approachable, educational content, as well as techniques and tools that accommodate a variety of physical abilities.
Project Overview
Women and others with limited physical strength often feel uncomfortable allowing service people into their homes or taking on home repair projects themselves because of a lack of knowledge or safety concerns. Our research led us to create a conceptual online toolbox of information and tips centered on alternate approaches for limited physical abilities that can empower women and others to tackle a home repair project on their own.

Our user interviews confirmed our initial research assumption, but we soon realized the problem we thought we identified was more complex. The insights hinted at the need for a different type of empowerment.

REALIZATION AND PROBLEM STATEMENT

After synthesizing our research we uncovered a new insight; that users often would like to take on these repair projects themselves. Because of a lack of knowledge about an approachable process and/or physical strength limitations, we realized these users were seeking a proactive solution to DIY home repairs tailored to their physical limitations. We asked ourselves how might we create a resource of information and tips centered on the abilities and alternate approaches of smaller body types to empower people to approach and tackle a home repair project on their own.

The Future of Lefty Lucy
Key Learnings
  1. Women are aware of risks involved when depending on service people, resulting in a desire to understand of the process of a repair and the skillset to take repairs into their own hands.
  2. During research, we were confronted by our own biases. Not only women would benefit from this app. Through user testing outside of our intended user, we saw that there is an unmet need and knowledge gap overall, particularly within the LGBTIQ+ community.
  3. Many competitor apps approach home repairs from the point of view of a homeowner even though many users live in rental properties and still have need to fix things around their home. Lefty Lucy’s content should include these users and tailor project instructions to allow users to remain compliant within their lease restrictions.
Future Development
  • Next iterations of this project will be to move into higher fidelity prototypes and the addition of the desktop site.
  • We envisioned future iterations of this tool including content created by trusted instructors, live virtual classes, and built-in shopping lists with live links to product sites like McMaster-Carr or a user’s local Home Depot.
